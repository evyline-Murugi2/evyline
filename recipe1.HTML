Kenyan Soft Layered Chapati

Ingredients (makes 8)

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Extra flour for dusting
  • 1/2 cup ghee or cooking oil (for frying)

Traditional Method

  1. Mix flour and salt in a bowl. Gradually add warm water to form soft dough.
  2. Knead for 10 minutes until smooth. Coat with 1 tbsp oil, cover with damp cloth, and rest for 30 minutes.
  3. Divide dough into 8 balls. Roll each into thin circles (6-8 inch diameter).
  4. Brush surface with ghee/oil, sprinkle flour, and roll into rope. Coil the rope tucking the end underneath.
  5. Rest coils for 10 minutes, then roll out again to 1/4 inch thickness.
  6. Cook on medium-hot dry pan for 30 seconds per side, then add ghee/oil and fry until golden with dark spots.
  7. Press edges gently with spatula to encourage layering.

Cultural Tip

Perfect chapatis should puff up with steam when cooking - this creates the signature layers. Serve with stew, tea, or sukuma wiki.
